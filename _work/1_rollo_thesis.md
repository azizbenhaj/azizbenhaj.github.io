# 🎓 Rollomatic SA – Data Engineer Intern & Master Thesis (7 Months)

## 📌 Transition to Research

After my initial internship period, I continued at **Rollomatic SA** for an additional **7 months**, during which I completed my **Master Thesis in Data Science** in collaboration with EPFL.

---

## 🎓 Master Thesis Project

### 📘 Title  
**Improved Curve Similarity Search for Cutting Tools: Faster Design and Optimized Tool Creation**

### 👨‍🏫 Supervision  
- Industrial Supervisor: Emna Maghrebi (Rollomatic SA)  
- Academic Supervisor: Prof. Jean-Philippe Thiran (EPFL)

---

## 🧩 Problem Statement

Industrial engineers rely on existing tools as references for new designs, but identifying similar tools is:

- Manual and time-consuming  
- Dependent on expert knowledge  
- Limited by heuristic similarity definitions  

The goal was to build a **learned similarity system for curve-based retrieval**.

---

## 🔬 Technical Work

- Built dataset of curve-level similarity annotations  
- Developed representation learning pipeline:
  - Classical shape descriptors  
  - Pretrained deep embeddings  
  - Metric learning models (contrastive, triplet, rank-aware learning)  
- Designed evaluation metrics for retrieval quality:
  - nDCG  
  - gMRR  
- Compared binary vs ranking-based similarity learning  

---

## 📊 Key Findings

- Classical descriptors remain strong baselines  
- Pretrained vision models alone are insufficient for fine-grained curve similarity  
- Binary contrastive learning improves classification but harms ranking quality  
- Rank-aware metric learning significantly improves retrieval performance  

---

## 💡 Impact

- Improved retrieval of geometrically similar cutting tool curves  
- Reduced dependency on manual expert selection  
- Established a scalable ML framework for industrial similarity search
